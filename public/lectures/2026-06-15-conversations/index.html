What strategies do you have for working with IT or Laboratory Leadership?

What has been the most effective and what has been the least effective?

Strategies for connecting with IT

  • Frame requests through their risk lens and identify how will you mitigate risk
    • How will security be managed?
    • Audit trails?
    • Software maintenance?
  • Shadow and observe their polices and procedures
    • How does your IT manage software and requests?
    • What does their project load look like?
    • In what ways can you better align bioinformatics operations to their best-practices
  • Shared monitoring
    • Does your bioinformatics approach appear as a black box to IT?

Strategies for connecting with Laboratory Leadership

  • Frame your work within the laboratories' typical assay validations
    • How will QC of pipelines align with laboratory standards?
    • What methods will be used to document software and pipelines?
    • When issues occur do you have a plan for continuity of operations?
  • Consider cost-benefits and long term stability
    • Between cloud and on-prem how do the costs breakdown?
    • In what ways does a custom designed strategy contrast to an "off-the-shelf" solution?
    • Describe the value-add?

Cross-cutting Strategies

  • Be mindful of overlapping terminology, a "test" has might have a different meaning between you, IT, and leadership.
  • Embed a liasion, having a designated person attending meetings builds translators who can shift expectations and contexts between worlds.
  • Communicate technical-debt without saying technical-debt, speed and quick deadlines can risk long term stability.
  • Think in terms of user stories, these help link a technical requirement to a goal:
    As a [public health epidemiologist], I need [automated lineage assignment] so that [we can meet the 24-hour reporting mandate], which requires [IT to open port 443 to GISAID and provision 64GB RAM nodes].

Scenario A

You need to purchase two Linux servers to run workflows and manage data stored on a network attached file system. However, the lab has only ever used Windows environments and no one has ever asked for a "Linux" before.

Scenario B

A recent respiratory outbreak in your state is suspected to be Nipah virus. The laboratory director is asking how soon WGS can brought up for NiV. How do you respond?.
